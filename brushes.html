Essential Makeup Brushes

1 / 10
Bronzer Brush:

Apply bronzer to the areas of the face the sun would naturally hit.

2 / 10
Buff Brush:

Apply eyeshadow to the crease of the eye and use the buff brush to blend out harsh lines to give a more natural look

3 / 10
Contour Brush:

Apply highlighter and countour to suitable areas of the face.
Blend to harsh lines are no more using the countour brush. Provides an airbrush effect to contour

4 / 10
Eye Line Brush:

Start in the middle. Place the brush against your upper lash line so that it is as close as possible to your eye lashes. Then, begin applying eyeliner to your upper lash line. Move out towards the outer edge of your lash line.

5 / 10
Foundation Brush:

Pour a small amount of foundation onto the brush.If you want fuller coverage, use both sides of the brush to pick up the makeup.Start applying the makeup in the center of the face so that you can blend outwards and avoid noticeable makeup lines

6 / 10
Fan Brush:

Distribute powdered foundation: Once you’ve applied your favorite foundation, use a fan brush with compact bristles to buff
out the powder for a perfectly blended and porcelain-looking complexion

7 / 10
Lip Brush:

Start from the center of your lips and blend product out towards your lip line. To get a crisp finish, use the tip of the brush
to define the lip lines at the bows and corners

8 / 10
Blush Brush:

Make strokes with the brush from your cheeks to the side of your face

9 / 10
Eye Brush:

Pat Glitter eyeshadow onto lid of the eye. Let eyeshadow dry and re-apply for a more dramatic glitter eye look

10 / 10
Definer Brush:

Swirl brush into eye shadow. Gently tap handle to remove excess.Work eye shadow into base of lashes and smudge. Continue all along lashline with short, even strokes.
